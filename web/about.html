Thomas Down

Creator of Dalliance. Developed Dazzle, one of the first implementation of a DAS server built on the BioJava library, which he co-founded in 2000 (Holland et al, 2008). He has been active in the development of the DAS protocol right up to the 1.53E (Jenkinson et al, 2008) and 1.6 versions today.

Matias Piipari

Has expertise in designing and writing usable software, both academic research tools (Piipari et al, 2010) and commercial applications.

Tim Hubbard

Bug finder and new feature proposer for Dalliance. Has been an advocate of DAS since its beginning, encouraging its adoption by Ensembl (Hubbard et al, 2002) and the efamily and biosapiens projects, under which Andreas Prlic developed the SPICE DAS client (Prlic et al, 2005) and the DAS registry (Prlic et al, 2007). He has had an interest in methods for layering annotation onto protein structures in a distributed way since the early days of the SCOP database (Hubbard, et al, 1995)
